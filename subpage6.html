Sambusa Recipe!



Ingredients:



For the Filling:


  1. 1 pound ground beef
  2. 1 large onion, finely chopped
  3. 2 cloves garlic, minced
  4. 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  5. 1/2 teaspoon ground coriander
  6. 1/4 teaspoon turmeric
  7. 1/4 teaspoon red pepper flakes (adjust to taste)
  8. 1/4 cup chopped fresh cilantro
  9. Salt and black pepper to taste
  10. 1 tablespoon olive oil
  11. For the Wrappers:
  12. 1 package wonton wrappers (about 30-36 wrappers)
  13. Water for sealing


Instructions:



Make the Filling:


Heat olive oil in a large skillet over medium heat. Add onion and garlic and sauté until softened. Add ground beef and cook until browned, breaking it up with a spoon. Stir in cumin, coriander, turmeric, red pepper flakes, and salt and pepper. Cook for a few more minutes until the spices are fragrant. Remove from heat and stir in chopped cilantro. Let cool slightly.

Assemble the Sambusa:


Lay a wonton wrapper on a clean surface. Place 1-2 tablespoons of filling in the center of the wrapper. Fold the wrapper in half diagonally to form a triangle. Moisten the edges with water. Press the edges together firmly to seal. Trim off any excess dough. Repeat with remaining wrappers and filling.

Fry the Sambusa:


Heat about 1 inch of oil in a deep fryer or large pot over medium-high heat. Fry the sambusa in batches for 5-7 minutes per batch, or until golden brown and crispy. Remove with a slotted spoon and drain on paper towels.

Serve:


Serve warm with your favorite dipping sauces, such as chutney, tamarind sauce, or yogurt sauce.

Tips & Variations:



Filling Variations:


Substitute ground beef with ground lamb, chicken, or vegetables (like potatoes, peas, and carrots). Add chopped spinach or other leafy greens to the filling. Use a combination of ground meat and vegetables.

Spice Level:


Adjust the amount of red pepper flakes to your preference. Baking: For a healthier option, you can bake the sambusa in a preheated oven at 400°F for 15-20 minutes, or until golden brown.

Freezing:


\ Assemble the sambusa and freeze them on a baking sheet. Once frozen, transfer them to an airtight container and store in the freezer for up to 3 months. Fry from frozen.
