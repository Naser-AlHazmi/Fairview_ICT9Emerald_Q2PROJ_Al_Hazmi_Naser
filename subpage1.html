Hummus Recipe!




Making homemade hummus involves a few key ingredients and steps, but the nuances and techniques can elevate the flavor and texture. Below is a detailed guide to preparing hummus from scratch, along with tips for perfecting it.

Ingredients:
  1. 1 cup dried chickpeas (garbanzo beans) or 2 cans (15 oz) cooked chickpeas
  2. 1 teaspoon baking soda (if using dried chickpeas)
  3. 1/2 cup tahini (well-stirred)
  4. 1/4 cup fresh lemon juice (about 1-2 lemons)
  5. 2-3 tablespoons olive oil (extra virgin, plus more for serving)
  6. 2 garlic cloves (crushed)
  7. 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  8. Salt (to taste)
  9. Water (as needed to adjust texture)
  10. Paprika (for garnish, optional)
  11. Chopped parsley (for garnish, optional)
  12. Food processor or blender (for smooth consistency)
  13. Large pot (if using dried chickpeas)
  14. Strainer (to rinse and drain chickpeas)

Procedure:

  1. Prepare the Chickpeas Using dried chickpeas (preferred for texture):

    Rinse the dried chickpeas and soak them in plenty of water for 12 hours or overnight. Drain and rinse again.
    Place the chickpeas in a large pot, cover with water, and add 1 teaspoon of baking soda. Bring to a boil, skim off any foam, then reduce the heat and simmer for 1.5–2 hours, or until they are very soft and slightly split.
    Drain the chickpeas, reserving a bit of the cooking liquid (about 1/4 cup).

    Using canned chickpeas:
    Drain and rinse the canned chickpeas thoroughly under cold water to remove excess salt and preservatives.


  2. Peel the Chickpeas (Optional but Highly Recommended)
    Peeling chickpeas can make your hummus extra smooth and creamy. To do this, gently squeeze each chickpea between your fingers to remove the skin. This step can take a bit of time, but it will improve the texture. Note: This step is optional but recommended for a smoother consistency.


  3. Blend the Tahini and Lemon Juice
    In the bowl of your food processor, combine the tahini and fresh lemon juice.
    Blend for about 30 seconds. Scrape down the sides and bottom of the bowl, then blend for another 30 seconds. This process emulsifies the tahini and lemon juice, making the hummus smoother.


  4. Add Garlic and Cumin
    Add the crushed garlic cloves and cumin to the tahini-lemon mixture.
    Blend for another 30 seconds until fully incorporated.


  5. Add the Chickpeas
    Add the chickpeas to the food processor (if using canned chickpeas, no need to drain them further, just add them straight).
    Process the mixture for 1-2 minutes until the chickpeas are broken down and blended in. Scrape down the sides as needed.


  6. Adjust Texture and Flavor
    With the food processor running, slowly add water (or reserved chickpea cooking liquid if you used dried chickpeas) in 1-2 tablespoon increments, processing until you reach your desired consistency. Start with 1/4 cup of water and gradually add more if necessary. The hummus should be smooth, but not too runny.
    Add salt to taste, blending again to incorporate.
    Optionally, drizzle in 2 tablespoons of olive oil for extra creaminess.


  7. Taste and Adjust Seasonings
    Taste the hummus and adjust the seasoning: more lemon juice for tanginess, garlic for sharpness, cumin for warmth, or salt for balance. Blend again to ensure all the ingredients are evenly incorporated.


  8. Serve and Garnish
    Transfer the hummus to a serving bowl.
    Drizzle a bit of olive oil on top and sprinkle with paprika, chopped parsley, or additional cumin if desired.
    For extra flair, you can also add pine nuts, roasted vegetables, or a few whole chickpeas on top.


Tips for Perfect Hummus:
Tahini: The quality of tahini significantly affects the flavor and texture of the hummus. Choose a good quality, creamy tahini that is smooth and not too bitter. Garlic: Adjust the garlic based on your preference for raw garlic's sharpness. Roasting the garlic first can make it milder.

Texture:
The key to smooth hummus is in the blending process. If the hummus feels grainy or thick, keep adding water or liquid slowly until you get a creamy texture.
Chickpeas: If you used canned chickpeas, draining and rinsing them well helps remove excess salt and can make your hummus taste fresher.

Serving Suggestions:
Serve hummus with pita bread, vegetable sticks (like carrots, cucumbers, or celery), or as a spread for wraps and sandwiches.

Variations:
  • Spicy Hummus: Add a pinch of cayenne pepper, chili flakes, or roasted red peppers to the blend for heat.
  • Herbed Hummus: Blend in fresh herbs like cilantro, mint, or basil for a fresh twist.
  • Roasted Garlic Hummus: For a deeper, sweeter garlic flavor, roast the garlic cloves in the oven (wrap in foil with a little olive oil and bake at 400°F for 30-40 minutes) before adding them to the mix.
