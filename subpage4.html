Biryani Recipe!



Ingredients:



For the Chicken Marinade:


  1. 1.5 lbs boneless, skinless chicken thighs or breasts, cut into 1-inch pieces
  2. 1 cup plain yogurt
  3. 1/4 cup lemon juice
  4. 2 tablespoons olive oil
  5. 2 cloves garlic, minced
  6. 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  7. 1/2 teaspoon ground coriander
  8. 1/4 teaspoon turmeric
  9. 1/4 teaspoon red chili powder (adjust to taste)
  10. 1/4 teaspoon garam masala
  11. Salt and black pepper to taste


For the Rice:


  1. 2 cups basmati rice, washed and soaked in water for 30 minutes
  2. 4 cups chicken broth
  3. 1 large onion, thinly sliced
  4. 2 cloves garlic, minced
  5. 1-inch piece ginger, grated
  6. 2 bay leaves
  7. 2 green cardamom pods
  8. 1 small cinnamon stick
  9. 1/4 teaspoon saffron strands, soaked in 2 tablespoons warm water
  10. 1/4 cup chopped fresh cilantro
  11. 1/4 cup chopped fresh mint
  12. 1/4 cup ghee or melted butter
  13. Salt to taste


For Garnishing:


  1. Fried onions
  2. Slivered almonds
  3. Fresh cilantro sprigs

Instructions:



Marinate the Chicken:


In a bowl, combine all the marinade ingredients. Add the chicken pieces and toss to coat evenly. Cover and marinate in the refrigerator for at least 2 hours, or preferably overnight.

Cook the Chicken:


Heat ghee or oil in a large pot or Dutch oven over medium heat. Add the marinated chicken pieces and cook until browned on all sides. Remove the chicken from the pot and set aside.

Sauté the Aromatics:


In the same pot, add sliced onions, garlic, and ginger. Sauté until the onions are softened and translucent.

Cook the Rice:


Add bay leaves, cardamom pods, and cinnamon stick to the pot. Pour in the chicken broth. Bring to a boil, then reduce heat to low, cover, and simmer for 10-15 minutes. Drain the soaked rice and add it to the simmering broth. Stir gently and bring back to a boil. Reduce heat to low, cover tightly, and simmer for 15-20 minutes, or until the rice is cooked through and the liquid is absorbed.

Layer and Steam:


Spread half of the cooked rice evenly in the pot. Top with the cooked chicken pieces. Spread the remaining rice over the chicken. Drizzle with the saffron water. Cover tightly with a lid or foil. Steam for 5-10 minutes over low heat, or until the flavors have melded.

Serve:


Gently fluff the rice with a fork. Garnish with fried onions, slivered almonds, and fresh cilantro sprigs. Serve hot with raita (yogurt dip) or salad.

Tips & Variations:



Spice Level:


Adjust the amount of red chili powder to your preference.

Meat Options:


Use lamb, beef, or even vegetables (like potatoes or cauliflower) instead of chicken.

Rice Variations:


Try using brown rice or a blend of basmati and brown rice for added nutrition.

Dum Method:


For extra flavorful rice, place a heatproof plate or inverted bowl on top of the pot after sealing it. This helps create steam and cooks the rice evenly.
