Kabsa Recipe!



Ingredients:

    For the Chicken:

  1. 1 (3-pound) whole chicken, cut into 8 pieces
  2. 1 large onion, chopped
  3. 6 cloves garlic, minced
  4. 1/4 cup tomato paste
  5. 1 (14.5 ounce) can diced tomatoes, undrained
  6. 3 medium carrots, peeled and grated
  7. 2 whole cloves
  8. 1 pinch ground nutmeg
  9. 1 pinch ground cumin
  10. 1 pinch ground coriander
  11. Salt and freshly ground black pepper to taste
  12. 1/4 cup butter


    For the Rice:

  1. 2 1/4 cups unrinsed basmati rice
  2. 1/4 cup raisins
  3. 1/4 cup slivered almonds, toasted
  4. 3 1/4 cups hot water
  5. 1 cube chicken bouillon


Kabsa Spice Mix:

  1. 1/2 teaspoon saffron threads
  2. 1/4 teaspoon ground green cardamom
  3. 1/2 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  4. 1/4 teaspoon ground allspice
  5. 1/4 teaspoon ground black pepper
  6. 1/4 teaspoon ground cloves


Instructions:



Prepare the Chicken:

In a large pot or Dutch oven, melt butter over medium heat. Add chicken pieces, onion, and garlic. Saut√© until onion is softened and chicken is lightly browned. Stir in tomato paste and cook for a few minutes more. Add diced tomatoes, carrots, cloves, nutmeg, cumin, coriander, salt, and pepper. Bring to a simmer, then reduce heat and cook for 20-25 minutes, or until chicken is almost cooked through.

Prepare the Rice:
In a small bowl, combine saffron threads with 1/4 cup hot water. Let it steep for 15 minutes. In a separate bowl, combine basmati rice with the Kabsa Spice Mix.

Assemble and Cook:
Remove chicken from the pot and set aside. Add the chicken broth and bouillon cube to the pot with the vegetables. Bring to a boil. Carefully spread the seasoned rice evenly over the vegetables in the pot. Top with the chicken pieces. Add the saffron water to the pot. Cover tightly with a lid or aluminum foil. Reduce heat to low and simmer for 15-20 minutes, or until rice is tender and all liquid is absorbed.

Rest and Serve:
Remove from heat and let the pot rest for 5-10 minutes to allow the flavors to meld. Fluff the rice with a fork. Garnish with raisins and toasted almonds. Serve hot with your choice of accompaniments (yogurt, salad, flatbread).

Tips and Variations:



Spice Levels:
Adjust the amount of spices in the Kabsa Spice Mix to your preference.

Meat Options:
Substitute chicken with lamb, beef, or even fish. Vegetable Additions: Include other vegetables such as peas, potatoes, or zucchini. Dum Method: For extra flavorful rice, place a heatproof plate or inverted bowl on top of the pot after sealing it. This helps create steam and cooks the rice evenly. Homemade Stock: Use homemade chicken or vegetable stock for richer flavor.
